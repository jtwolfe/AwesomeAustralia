# AwesomeAustralia

**Redesigning Australia from the ground up for high-agency, high-trust, high-abundance citizens.**

This is a living documentation repository for evidence-based systems of governance, economic architecture, social programs, and long-term trajectories toward post-scarcity. Grounded in incentives, selection effects, human nature, Australia’s resource and geographic realities, and acceleration of beneficial technological progress.

We reject both unlimited population importation and extreme remigration fantasies. We aim for pragmatic, high-selection, high-responsibility systems that maximise real autonomy, opportunity, and surplus for Australian citizens now and in the future.

## Core Vision

A society in which:
- The coercive state is minimal and high-capacity (defence, basic order, critical infrastructure coordination).
- Material scarcity in food, energy, and housing is largely deleted through technology + institutions.
- People are free, possess rights, and fulfil reciprocal responsibilities.
- High performers and those who strive are rewarded; baseline contribution is expected; predation is constrained.
- Cultural continuity and social trust are treated as public goods maintained by selection and transmission, not slogans.
- Progress accelerates toward greater abundance, agency, and frontiers (including space), inspired by frames like Iain M. Banks’ Culture while remaining grounded in physics and incentives.

**Accelerationism here means**: deliberately front-loading high-leverage reforms and technology deployment (energy abundance, AI/robotics clearance, housing elasticity, resource rent capture) so that compounding gains arrive sooner, while preserving the high-agency cultural substrate that makes abundance liberating rather than dystopian.

## Measurable Goals (Citizen-Centric)

1. **Per-capita prosperity**: Sustained multifactor productivity growth >1–1.5%; rising real median living standards leading advanced peers.
2. **Housing attainability**: National median dwelling price-to-income ratio sustainably 4–6×; highly elastic supply.
3. **Demographic sustainability**: Path to higher fertility (target direction 1.7–1.9+) via lower costs of family formation; population growth weighted to natural increase + high-selectivity migration.
4. **High social trust & low parallel societies**: Recovery in belonging, interpersonal trust, national pride metrics; integration success as binding constraint on migration volume.
5. **Competent non-captured institutions**: High state capacity in core functions; selection for competence and long time-horizons.
6. **Resource & energy abundance**: Maximise citizen share of resource rents (Norway-style SWF + dividends potential); cheap, reliable energy as foundation.
7. **High agency**: Freedom → Rights → Responsibilities. Voluntary excellence matched to aptitude and passion on top of a lean core state.

## Key Design Principles

- Incentives and selection over stated intentions.
- Per-capita metrics primary; aggregate GDP secondary.
- Housing supply elasticity is non-negotiable for any sustainable population policy.
- Migration volume and composition constrained by objective absorption capacity (housing, infrastructure, fiscal contribution, cohesion).
- Explicit trade-offs. Feedback loops that force correction.
- Minimal coercive core + maximal voluntary/competitive provision of everything else.
- Cultural transmission of reciprocity, low time-preference, and excellence.
