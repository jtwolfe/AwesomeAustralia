# Governance Architecture

## Minimal Coercive Core
Strip the federal government to non-optional functions:
- Defence of the continent.
- Federal police and courts to enforce contracts and punish aggression.
- Minimal tax mechanism to fund only the above.
- Bare-bones authority over critical interstate freight corridors (ports, major roads/rail arteries).

Everything else is optional and can be provided privately, mutually, competitively by states, or not at all. Realistic costing is higher than pure night-watchman ideals (current defence alone is tens of billions and rising), but the principle holds: fund only pure public goods that markets and voluntary action systematically under-provide, and fund them leanly.

## Federalism Reform
Dramatically reduce vertical fiscal imbalance. Give states stronger own-source revenue (income tax sharing or land-based revenues) so that jurisdictions delivering better housing, infrastructure, and services face clearer rewards and competitive pressure.

## Competence Selection
Higher compensation and rigorous cognitive + character selection for elite civil service and key operational roles (Singapore lessons, adapted). Rotation, performance accountability, reduced purely political appointments in delivery roles.

## Long-Termism Mechanisms
Independent statutory bodies with real teeth on migration volume and housing supply targets (tied to objective metrics; high-threshold parliamentary override). Explore elements of direct democracy or citizen initiatives for major irreversible commitments, with double-majority protections. Constitutional or hard statutory limits on expansion of the federal core.

## Voluntary Layer
Once the coercive minimum is fixed and small, layer passion-matched, high-agency contribution: militia-style participation with status and transferable skills; extreme cognitive selection for consistency-critical roles (e.g., certain judicial functions); enthusiast-led innovation in infrastructure and culture where network externalities allow.
