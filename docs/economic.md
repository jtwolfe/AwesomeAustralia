# Economic Architecture

## Housing & Land Use
Radical supply-side reform: by-right mid-density in well-located areas, concurrent infrastructure funding, reduced discretionary planning that creates artificial scarcity. Explore land-value elements in taxation to discourage pure speculation while preserving strong property rights. Phase carefully to manage wealth effects and political resistance.

## Migration Economics
Volume dynamically linked to housing completions + vacancy rates + infrastructure metrics. Heavy points-based selection optimised for long-term net fiscal contribution, age, language, skills, and predicted integration. Strict temporary vs permanent distinction. End or tightly constrain pathways that dilute average contribution.

## Resource Rents
Move toward Norway-style capture of pure resource rents (high effective taxation of economic rents + equity stakes) channelled into a genuine sovereign wealth fund that invests primarily abroad and only spends expected real returns. Potential for citizen dividends as surplus grows. Current arrangements under-capture relative to potential.

## Productivity & Energy
Energy abundance (reliable, low-cost, high-density power) is the master resource. Nuclear (including advanced/SMR), gas bridge, and whatever renewables + storage deliver at system scale. Competition policy, skills formation matched to high-value demand, reduced regulatory burden on productive investment, public-sector efficiency.

## Tax Architecture
Aim for radically lower, simpler, less distortionary taxation. Residual core state funded preferentially from resource rents and broad bases that minimise deadweight loss (land value elements, consumption, user fees). Competitive federalism so jurisdictions that deliver better outcomes attract people and capital. Near-zero tax is aspirational; pure zero fails the public-goods problem.

## Industry
Leverage resources and energy for competitive advantage. Value-add where economically rational. Advanced manufacturing and high-productivity services where energy, skills, and location allow. Avoid forced industrial policy that ignores comparative advantage.
