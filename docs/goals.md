# Goals

Citizen-centric, measurable targets for the redesign. These are directional and subject to refinement with better data and modelling.

## 1. Per-Capita Prosperity
- Multifactor productivity growth sustainably above 1.0–1.5% per year.
- Rising real median wages and consumption leading advanced-economy peers.
- Focus on median and lower-middle outcomes, not just averages or aggregates.

## 2. Housing Attainability
- National median dwelling price-to-income ratio in the 4–6× range (historically normal / internationally balanced markets are ~3–5×).
- Rents a significantly lower share of household income (target direction <25%).
- Housing supply highly elastic so that demand shocks (including moderated population growth) do not produce multi-year price explosions.

## 3. Demographic Sustainability
- Path toward total fertility rate recovery in the 1.7–1.9+ range, driven primarily by lower costs of family formation (housing first).
- Net overseas migration volume dynamically constrained by housing completions, infrastructure capacity, and social absorption metrics.
- Composition heavily weighted to high long-term fiscal contribution, language, skills, and integration potential.

## 4. High Social Trust and Cohesion
- Measurable recovery in interpersonal trust, sense of belonging, and national pride (Scanlon-style and World Values Survey metrics).
- Low formation of parallel societies that reject the core operating system of reciprocity, rule of law, and English-language high-trust coordination.
- Integration success treated as a binding constraint on volume.

## 5. Competent, Non-Captured Institutions
- High state capacity concentrated in core functions (defence, justice, critical infrastructure coordination).
- Selection mechanisms that favour cognitive competence, character, and long time-horizons over short-term political or interest-group capture.
- Reduced vertical fiscal imbalance and stronger competitive federalism.

## 6. Resource and Energy Abundance
- Maximise the citizen share of pure resource rents via reformed taxation / equity stakes channelled into a genuine sovereign wealth fund (Norway model adapted) with potential for citizen dividends.
- Cheap, reliable, high-density energy as the foundation for industry, living standards, and further automation.

## 7. High Agency and Responsibility
- Freedom as baseline for competent adults.
- Rights that protect spheres of action and non-interference.
- Reciprocal responsibilities: non-aggression, contribution to common infrastructure, transmission of norms that sustain trust.
- Graduated response to social-contract failure: temporary support that preserves agency, or constraint when harm is imposed on others.
- Voluntary excellence and passion-matched contribution layered on a lean coercive core.

## 8. Acceleration Toward Greater Abundance
- Front-load high-leverage reforms and technology deployment so that material scarcities in food, energy, and housing are deleted as rapidly as physics and institutions allow.
- Maintain the cultural and institutional substrate that turns material abundance into expanded human possibility rather than new dependency or control.

These goals are interdependent. Housing elasticity unlocks family formation and reduces political pressure for coercive redistribution. Energy and productivity abundance fund the residual state and private mutual aid. Selection and cultural transmission sustain the trust that makes low-coercion systems viable.
