# Social & Human Capital Architecture

## Freedom, Rights, Responsibilities in Practice
Private voluntary help (including to the homeless) should face near-zero friction. Abundance makes mutual aid scalable. The hard edge is harm to others: when impairment or addiction produces violence or sustained destruction of public space, constraint is legitimate. Housing First + treatment for many complex needs; selective institutionalisation for the dangerous.

## Family Formation
Housing affordability is the dominant lever for fertility recovery. Targeted supports can help at the margin but do not substitute for lower housing and opportunity costs.

## Education & Culture
High standards, cognitive skill focus, shared civic and historical literacy. Reduce ideological capture. Merit-based pathways. Cultural hierarchy of quality is legitimate and healthy (“nothing forbidden, but some things are less serious, less skilled, or less true”).

## Welfare & Integration
Work requirements, time limits, and asset tests that preserve incentives. Real integration requirements (language, economic self-sufficiency, basic civic knowledge). Selection quality and volume relative to absorption capacity are the primary tools against parallel societies.

## Cohesion
Treat high-trust reciprocity norms and English as the high-trust coordination language as public goods. Preference in selection for cohorts with proven rapid integration track records. Status, recognition, and belonging for those who contribute and transmit the operating system.
