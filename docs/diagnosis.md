# Diagnosis (Status circa mid-2026)

## Key Empirical Patterns

- **Net Overseas Migration**: Peaked ~538k (2022–23), still ~300–306k in 2024–25 / year to Dec 2025. Pre-COVID normal range roughly 180–260k. Population growth is overwhelmingly migration-driven; natural increase ~110k.
- **Housing**: National median dwelling-to-income ratios ~8.2× (Cotality/Demographia range), Sydney substantially higher. Severely unaffordable by historical and international standards (balanced markets typically 3–5×). Supply highly inelastic due to zoning, planning, and infrastructure lag. Household debt-to-income elevated (~177%).
- **Productivity**: Multifactor productivity weak or negative recently; long-run labour productivity growth well below 1990s–early 2000s reform era.
- **Economic structure**: Services-dominant; mining ~10% of output but majority of merchandise exports; manufacturing share collapsed to ~5%.
- **Fertility**: TFR ~1.48 (2024) and trending lower. Far below replacement.
- **Cohesion**: Scanlon Mapping Social Cohesion index relatively stable but below longer-term peaks; belonging and national pride metrics have declined; majority concerns about migration levels often driven by housing and economic pressures.
- **Fiscal of migration**: Skilled permanent streams typically lifetime net positive (Treasury FIONA-style modelling); family and humanitarian less so or negative. Models under-count housing, congestion, and infrastructure externalities.

## Root Causes (Incentive & Selection Focused)

1. Inelastic housing supply + high and poorly timed demand growth (including migration volume).
2. Political incentives that reward aggregate GDP and population headlines over per-capita outcomes and cohesion.
3. Incomplete capture of resource rents for citizens.
4. Regulatory and institutional arrangements favouring short-termism and capture over competence and long horizons.
5. Fertility suppressed by the cost of family formation (housing is the largest lever).

This is a coherent set of incentive failures and selection problems, not a conspiracy.
