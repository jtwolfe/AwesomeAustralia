# Long-Term Energy Demand: 30–50 Year Utopia Scenarios

**Goal**: In 30–50 years, electricity (and broader energy services) should cost *basically nothing* for citizens — near-zero marginal cost abundance that removes energy as a constraint on agency, industry, computation, and living standards.

This requires both massive scale-up of low-marginal-cost generation and deliberate overbuild so that scarcity rents are rare.

## Current Baseline (mid-2020s)

- NEM grid electricity consumption: ~200–210 TWh/year.
- Broader Australian electricity generation (including rooftop and non-NEM): roughly 250–300 TWh class.
- Primary energy consumption: ~130 Mtoe (~1,500 TWh thermal equivalent).
- Per-capita electricity use is already high by global standards but has room to rise with full electrification and new high-intensity uses.

## Official Trajectory (AEMO-style to 2050)

AEMO’s 2026 Integrated System Plan and related modelling project:

- NEM underlying electricity consumption nearly doubles to approximately **390 TWh by 2050**.
- Drivers: electrification of transport, industrial processes, heating; population and economic growth; emerging data-centre demand (potentially reaching ~10% of underlying demand in higher scenarios).
- Business/industry grid electricity roughly doubles; household *net* grid demand falls due to rooftop solar self-consumption.
- Total system capacity (generation + storage) more than triples in some pathways.

This is a *managed transition* forecast under current policy and technology cost trajectories. It is **not** an abundance or utopia scenario.

## Drivers of Higher Demand Under AwesomeAustralia Goals

1. **Full electrification** of remaining fossil end-uses (transport, industry, buildings).
2. **AI and compute scale**: Data centres and training/inference loads. Global projections already show data-centre electricity potentially doubling or more by 2030; cheap, reliable Australian power could attract a disproportionate share.
3. **Robotics and automation**: Embodied AI and industrial robots raise electricity intensity of the economy while raising productivity.
4. **High living standards and agency**: More energy services per person (comfort, mobility, materials, leisure, self-actualisation activities).
5. **New industries enabled by cheap power**: Desalination at scale, synthetic fuels/chemicals, advanced materials, vertical or controlled-environment agriculture if needed, potential energy-intensive exports (green iron, ammonia, compute).
6. **Population**: Moderate growth under high-selection policy (target range perhaps 30–40 million rather than rapid expansion). Per-capita intensity dominates aggregate demand.
7. **Space and frontier activities**: Longer-term, launch, in-space manufacturing precursors, or related ground infrastructure.

Efficiency gains will offset some growth, but historical patterns and new use-cases (especially AI and electrification) typically drive absolute electricity demand upward in high-income settings when energy is abundant and cheap.

## Scenario Framework (Electricity, Australia-wide)

Approximate order-of-magnitude ranges for **annual electricity demand** (TWh). These are illustrative models for planning, not precise forecasts.

### 30-year horizon (~2055)

| Scenario              | Demand (TWh) | Multiple of today | Character |
|-----------------------|--------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Official / Baseline   | 400–500     | ~1.5–2×          | AEMO-like + moderate AI |
| Abundance             | 700–1,200   | ~3–4×            | Strong electrification, significant AI/compute, industrial reboot, higher per-capita services |
| High-Agency Utopia    | 1,500–2,500+| ~5–8×+           | Energy no longer a constraint; heavy compute, robotics, materials abundance, possible export of energy-intensive goods/services |

### 50-year horizon (~2075)

Further scaling is highly uncertain and depends on technological breakthroughs (e.g., radical efficiency in compute, molecular manufacturing, or fusion/very-low-cost firm power). Plausible ranges under continued abundance orientation:

- Abundance continuation: 1,500–3,000 TWh
- Radical post-scarcity trajectory: several thousand TWh or higher if energy becomes the substrate for widespread physical and computational abundance.

Per-capita electricity could rise from current high-income levels (~8–12 MWh/person) toward 20–50+ MWh/person or more in a high-agency, high-compute, highly automated society.

## Achieving “Basically Nothing” Cost

Near-zero *marginal* cost for users requires:

1. **Dominant low- or zero-fuel-cost generation** (nuclear, solar, wind).
2. **Deliberate overbuild** of capacity relative to peak demand so that energy is rarely the binding constraint. Curtailment of excess variable generation becomes acceptable.
3. **Abundant firming and storage** (batteries, pumped hydro, nuclear load-following, demand response, synthetic fuels) so reliability is maintained without scarcity pricing.
4. **Capital cost recovery** spread over enormous volumes of energy and long asset lives, ideally with low cost of capital (sovereign or citizen-oriented financing).
5. **System design** that prioritises abundance over minimal capital expenditure. The goal shifts from “cheapest possible MWh under scarcity” to “energy services so plentiful that price signals for conservation largely disappear for ordinary use.”

In such a regime, the *average* system cost can still be non-zero (capital must be recovered), but the *marginal* price faced by citizens and most industry approaches zero for the large majority of hours. This is the practical meaning of “power costs basically nothing.”

Nuclear (large or modular) provides high-capacity-factor firm power that reduces the total overbuild required compared with a pure variable + storage system. Solar and wind provide the lowest marginal-cost bulk energy. The optimal mix under an abundance objective is likely a combination that maximises reliability and minimises the frequency of scarcity events.

## Implications for Near-Term Strategy

- Front-load energy abundance now (Stage 0) so that the capital stock and institutional capability exist when demand ramps.
- Treat energy cost and reliability as first-order inputs to housing, industry, AI, and fertility goals.
- Plan transmission, sites, workforce, and regulatory pathways for multi-hundred to multi-thousand TWh scale rather than incremental replacement of the current system.
- Preserve and expand the nuclear option precisely because firm, high-density power becomes more valuable as absolute demand and the value of reliability rise.

## Next Modelling Steps

- More granular sectoral breakdown (residential, transport, industry, data centres, new industries).

- Linkage to resource rents / sovereign wealth mechanisms that can finance the capital stock while delivering near-zero marginal prices to citizens.

Energy abundance is not a side constraint on the utopia. It is one of the primary enablers.
