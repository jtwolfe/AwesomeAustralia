# Design Principles

## Core Philosophical Frame: Freedom → Rights → Responsibilities

- **Freedom** is the baseline condition of competent adults.
- **Rights** are the protected spheres of action and non-interference (property, speech, association, bodily integrity, exit) that make freedom real.
- **Responsibilities** are the reciprocal duties that keep the system from collapsing into predation or free-riding: do not aggress, do not impose uncompensated costs, contribute to the common infrastructure that makes high freedom possible, transmit the norms that sustain trust.

When people fail responsibilities, the response is graduated:
- Temporary or voluntary support that preserves agency and does not trap people in dependency.
- Clear, predictable constraint when the failure imposes harm on others (violence, chronic predation, destruction of public goods).
- Institutionalisation or incapacity responses only for those whose impairment makes them dangerous or incapable of basic reciprocity, with due process.

## Operating Principles

1. **Incentives and selection over intentions.** Design for human nature as it is.
2. **Per-capita metrics primary; aggregate GDP secondary.**
3. **Housing supply elasticity is non-negotiable** for any sustainable population or family policy.
4. **Migration volume and composition constrained by objective absorption capacity** (housing completions, infrastructure lag, fiscal contribution, cohesion metrics).
5. **Explicit trade-offs.** No free lunches. Build feedback loops that force correction.
6. **Minimal coercive core + maximal voluntary and competitive provision** of everything else.
7. **Cultural continuity and social trust as public goods** maintained primarily by selection at the border and transmission, not by narrative alone.
8. **Accelerate beneficial technology deployment** (energy, AI, robotics, precision agriculture) while preserving high-agency humans as the point of the system.
9. **Competence selection** in critical roles (justice, defence leadership, key public institutions).
10. **Resource rents treated as common heritage** to be captured for citizens and future generations rather than dissipated.

## Against Extremes

We reject both unlimited high-volume migration as moral destiny and extreme remigration framed as purification. Both treat people as abstractions. Functional redesign sits in the difficult middle: high selection, high expectations of contribution and cultural compatibility, high individual autonomy, and hard edges only where the social contract is broken by predation or free-riding that imposes costs on others.
